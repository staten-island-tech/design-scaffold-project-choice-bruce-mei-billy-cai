Attractions

The Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty was a gift from France to the United States as a sign of friendship. As one of the most recognizable statures in the country, it is often seen as a symbol of freedom and justice. Fully established on October 28, 1886, it stands at a whopping 305 feet and resides in Liberty Island located in New York. Incredible views from all angles, you may even be able to get your hands on a ticket and climb up to the crown of the Statue. This incredible monument strikes pride, hope, and inspiration in those who stop by.

Times Square

Times Square is a wonder to behold when visiting New York City. It is a major tourist destination for those who want to be enetertained, shop, and explore. Located in midtown Manhattan, Times Square is filled with those who come to see annual festivials/events especially during New Years. Every direction you turn there are numerous buildings, billboard, and lights. If you want something, chance has it that its in Time Square, from a wide arrangements of foods and fun. An estimated 50 million visitors come annually which is why Times Square is referred to as “the Crossroads of the World”.

The Museum of Natural History

The world's most popular natural history museum is dedicated to understanding the natural world and our place in it. Delve into the fascinating story of our planet, from its fiery beginnings through billions of years of transformation, and explore life on Earth through exhibitions and activities, collection objects and research that happens in the lab and in the field. The museum is larger than 18 football fields and is home to the largest natural history collection in the world.

Central Park

Arguably one of the most famous parks in the world, Central Park is a manmade wonder. Not only is it the first public park built in America, but it is also one of the most frequently visited, with over 25 million guests per year. Set in the middle of bustling Manhattan, its grounds serve as a safe haven, not only for athletes, daydreamers, musicians, and strollers, but also for teems of migratory birds each year. One can spend an entire peaceful day roaming its grounds, gazing upon nearly 50 fountains, monuments, and sculptures or admiring its 36 bridges and arches.

Food

The Bacon, Egg & Cheese

One of New York City's culinary mascots—the bacon, egg, and cheese is eaten by millions of New Yorkers every morning. A staple breakfast cherished all throughout the state, it is simple yet delicious. As the name implies, the sandwich/bagel is complimented with crispy bacon, eggs, and cheese. Served fresh and hot, the breakfast can be bought and enjoyed at many deli's, cafe's, and restaurants.

Eggs Benedict

The Eggs Benedict is a traditional American breakfast and brunch recipe that originated in New York City. It is made up of an toasted English muffin, topped with Canadian bacon, poached eggs, and classic French Hollandaise sauce. It's decadent, rich, and satisfying. Eggs Benedict can be found in a variety of restaurants and bakeries.

Pastrami Sandwich

Pastrami on rye bread is a classic sandwich made famous in the Jewish kosher delicatessens of New York City. It was first created in 1888 by Sussman Volk, who served it at his deli on Delancey Street in New York City. The sandwhich is made up of Pastrami, Rye Bread, and Mustard. Pastrami Sandwiches and Pastrami meat can be bought at Deli's and markets that sell different kinds of meat.

NYC Pizza

New York–style pizza is made with a characteristically large hand-tossed thin crust, often sold in wide slices to go. The crust is thick and crisp along its edge, yet soft, thin, and pliable enough beneath its toppings to be folded in half to eat. Traditionally, the main toppings consist of tomato sauce and shredded mozzarella cheese. You can find these pizzas almost anywhere in NYC, sold in many restaurants and pizzerias.

Burgers

Without a doubt, NYC provides some of the most prolific and quality burger joints in the world. Consisting of a freshly baked bun, a large juicy patty, cheese, accompanied by whatever suites your liking, a burger in NYC is definately one food you cant pass up. If you ever stop by and visit NYC, you should definately consider a burger as a must-eat.

Cheesecake

Fluffy, rich, and smooth; Cheesecake in NYC is a dessert to be cherished. With multiple dozens of varieties, there is at least a few that would catch your liking. It is sweet and consists of multiple layers of cheese and a pastry crust/base which is often made from crushed cookies, graham crackers, or sometimes sponge cake. Cheesecake can be found in many NYC restaurants, Cafes, and Bakeries.
